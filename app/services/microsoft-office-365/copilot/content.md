What Microsoft 365 Copilot Actually Does

Copilot runs in the heart of the Office apps. It’s not a chatbot sitting in a corner; it works with your actual files, emails, meetings, and calendars. Here’s what it looks like in action:

Microsoft Word
Copilot in Word can draft entire documents from a single prompt, rewrite your content to match a different tone or length, summarise pages into a punchy list, and pull in details from your other Microsoft 365 files. This is a game-changer if your team churns out proposals, policies, or reports. And if you’ve already set up branded templates, Copilot works with those too. Our Word experts can set things up so your branding and Copilot never clash.

Microsoft Excel
In Excel, Copilot helps your staff dig into data with natural language questions, build formulas without memorising syntax, create PivotTables on the fly, spot trends, and flag outliers. Suddenly, the power of Excel isn’t limited to the office spreadsheet guru—anyone can ask Copilot for a breakdown and get an answer in plain English. If your spreadsheets already include macros or dashboards, our consultants make sure Copilot slides in seamlessly.

Microsoft PowerPoint
Copilot in PowerPoint builds entire slide decks from basic prompts or Word documents, suggests better layouts, writes up speaker notes, and reorganises old presentations. If your team spends too much time fiddling with formatting, Copilot lets them focus on the story. Already using custom slide designs? Copilot sticks to your look and feel.

Microsoft Outlook
Copilot in Outlook digests long email threads, drafts replies based on the conversation, sorts your inbox by what matters, and lifts out action items. If email eats up a chunk of your day, Copilot saves you from drowning in messages.

Microsoft Teams
Teams is where Copilot shows its magic right away. It can summarise meetings as they happen, generate recaps with action items and who said what, and let you search missed meetings via the transcript. No more “Who took notes?” moments—Copilot captures the key decisions for you.

Copilot Licensing for Australian Businesses

Microsoft 365 Copilot is an extra licence you add to your existing Microsoft 365 plan. Here’s how licensing works in 2026:

You need a qualifying plan—Business Basic, Business Standard, Business Premium, E3, or E5—plus the Copilot add-on. Pricing starts around AU$27 per user per month (excluding GST), billed annually. Volume discounts or enterprise deals can shift the numbers, so we’ll get you a tailored quote.

Copilot Chat vs Microsoft 365 Copilot
There’s a free option: Copilot Chat comes with every Microsoft 365 business account. That gives you AI chat with web data—handy for questions and simple tasks, but it doesn’t tap into your actual organisational data or the Office apps themselves. The full Copilot licence adds proper integration—your files, your emails, your meetings, all accessible from inside Word, Excel, PowerPoint, and Outlook.

Copilot Studio access
Every Microsoft 365 Copilot licence includes Copilot Studio, where you can build your own AI agents. If you want to automate complex business processes or set up specialised bots, our Power Platform team is here to help. We go deep on Copilot Studio as part of our Power Platform consulting.
